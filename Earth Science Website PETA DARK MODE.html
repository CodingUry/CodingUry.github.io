Rising sea levels

The effects of our glaciers and ice caps melting do not only affect a singular area but the whole world, as they are responsible for rising sea levels that, when time passes, can submerge lowlands to their depths. Not only that, but it can elevate storm surges as warming air and ocean temperatures create more frequent and intense coastal storms like hurricanes and typhoons. The disappearance of glaciers also has a huge impact on our climate, for glaciers have white surfaces that reflect the sun's rays. This helps keep our current climate mild or ‘do-able’ as of the moment for more world problems to affect our climate. Although when glaciers melt, darker surfaces are exposed, which absorb heat. This raises temperatures even more. What’s more saddening is its effect on arctic animals like the polar bear, walruses, arctic fox, and many more, thus its effect on us humans.
